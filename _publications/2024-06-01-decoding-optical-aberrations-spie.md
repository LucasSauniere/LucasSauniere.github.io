We address the challenge of characterizing optical aberrations in **low-resolution space instruments** — a particularly difficult problem due to the limited spatial information available in undersampled PSFs.

Our approach combines:
- **Zernike polynomial decomposition** to parameterize wavefront aberrations in an interpretable basis
- **Convolutional neural networks** trained to estimate Zernike coefficients directly from observed PSFs
- **Validation on Euclid NISP** simulated and flight data

This work was presented as both an oral paper and a poster at **SPIE Astronomical Telescopes + Instrumentation 2024** in Yokohama, Japan.
